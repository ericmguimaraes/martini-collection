# Deep Collection Analysis — The Martini Collection

> A data-driven exploration of 1,687 CDs and 507 DVDs, uncovering hidden patterns,
> temporal evolution, and the bridges between music and cinema.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Martini Collection is not a casual accumulation — it is a curated cultural archive. The data reveals a collector whose taste is anchored in **jazz improvisation** and **Brazilian musical identity**, complemented by a cinema library that favors **auteur-driven drama**, **classic-era filmmaking**, and **international perspectives**.

Key findings:
- **Jazz-Latin-Bossa nexus**: The strongest genre co-occurrence in the CD collection is Latin + MPB (93 CDs), followed by Jazz + Latin (52). This triangle — jazz, Latin rhythms, and Brazilian popular music — is the collection's musical DNA.
- **Label identity is sharply defined**: Blue Note = 97% Jazz, Verve = 100% Jazz, Royal Philharmonic Orchestra = 100% Classical. Labels are not just distributors — they are taste markers.
- **Deep over broad**: 667 artists have just 1 album, but 10 artists have 11+ albums. The collection values depth with favorites while continuously exploring.
- **Cinema quality is remarkably consistent**: Average IMDb rating stays between 7.4–7.9 across every decade from the 1920s to 2010s. The collector doesn't compromise on quality regardless of era.
- **9 composers bridge both worlds**: Nino Rota alone connects 1 CD to 11 DVDs (from Fellini to The Godfather). Music and cinema are not separate interests — they are one interconnected sensibility.

## 6. Collector Archetype

**The Curated Cosmopolitan**: A collector who approaches both music and cinema as interconnected art forms, with three defining characteristics:

1. **Depth over breadth**: 71% of artists have only 1 album, but the top 10 artists average 15+ albums each. When something resonates, the collection goes deep — not just a best-of, but the full journey.

2. **Quality as non-negotiable**: An average IMDb rating of 7.6 across 507 DVDs, with remarkable consistency (7.4–7.9) across every decade. The collection doesn't contain guilty pleasures — every film earned its place.

3. **Brazilian cultural anchor**: Música Brasileira is the second-largest CD tag (395 albums, 23%), and Brazilian artists form the densest cross-collection network. The Tropicália movement (Gil, Caetano, Bethânia, Chico) appears as the single most interconnected cluster across CDs and DVDs.

4. **The Jazz-Cinema sensibility**: Jazz and classic cinema share an aesthetic — improvisation, atmosphere, emotional subtlety. The collection reflects someone who values the same qualities in both art forms: craft over spectacle, composition over formula, history over trends.
